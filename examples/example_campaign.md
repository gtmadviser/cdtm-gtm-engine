# Example output — what "done" looks like

A fictional team building **BookFlow**, an online-booking + no-show-reminder
tool for small service businesses. Use this as a shape reference, not a script.

---

## output/icp.md

> We target **owner-operators of 3–15 person service businesses** (physio,
> beauty, dental) in DACH **who still take bookings by phone and lose evenings
> to no-shows**. The trigger we hunt is a **strong review profile (4.5+ stars,
> 50+ reviews) but no online booking link** — proof of demand they can't fully
> capture. We detect it via Google Maps + their website. We reach them by email
> to the practice owner. We skip chains and anything over 20 staff. North-star:
> **demos booked / 100 sends.**

## output/campaign.md

```
# Campaign: BookFlow — service SMB no-show
North-star metric: demos booked / 100 sends
ICP: owner-operators of small DACH service businesses, great reviews, phone-only booking

## Step 1 (Day 1)
Subject A: volle wartezimmer, leere abende?
Subject B: ihre 4,8 sterne, ohne online-buchung?

Bei 120 Bewertungen rufen Ihre Patienten gern an, aber jeder No-Show kostet
einen ganzen Termin-Slot. Die meisten Praxen Ihrer Groesse verlieren so 4 bis 6
Stunden pro Woche.

BookFlow gibt Ihren Patienten einen Buchungslink mit automatischer Erinnerung,
Sie muessen nichts umstellen. Waere ein kurzer Blick naechste Woche interessant?

{{signature}}

## Step 2 (Day 4)
Subject: Re: ihre 4,8 sterne, ohne online-buchung?

Kurzer Nachfass: eine Praxis in Muenchen hat No-Shows in 3 Wochen halbiert,
nur durch die automatische Erinnerung. 10 Minuten, um zu sehen ob das passt?

{{signature}}
```

Note the rules in action: scenario opener, no em-dashes, under 70 words,
soft CTA, formal *Sie*, signature placeholder, follow-up adds a new angle
instead of repeating features.
