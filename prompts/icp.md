# ICP definition

Goal: go from a vague market to a **sharp** ideal customer profile with a trigger. Sharper ICP makes every downstream step easier.

## The framework — answer these

1. **Who, specifically?** Industry/vertical + company size + role of the person who feels the pain and can say yes. Not "SMBs" — "owner of a 5–20 person dental practice in DACH."
2. **What pain, in their words?** The problem as *they* would describe it, not your feature.
3. **The trigger — why now?** A signal that this account needs you *this week*:
   - hiring for a role your product touches
   - recently funded / expanding
   - uses a specific tool (complementary or a competitor)
   - a regulation, season, or event that just hit them
   - a recent review, launch, or public complaint
4. **Where do they congregate?** One channel you can actually reach them on.
5. **Disqualifiers.** Who looks like a fit but isn't (too big, wrong geo, no budget).

## Output (write to `output/icp.md`)

One tight paragraph, e.g.:

> We target **[who]** who **[pain]**. The trigger we hunt is **[signal]**, which we can detect via **[source]**. We reach them on **[channel]**. We skip **[disqualifier]**. Our north-star metric is **[metric]**.

Keep it to 4–6 sentences. If you can't name a trigger, the ICP isn't sharp enough yet.
